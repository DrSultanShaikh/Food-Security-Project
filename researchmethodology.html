Research Methodology

Advanced approaches for sustainable aquaculture development in Qatar

Methodological Framework

The project will transition from batch/fed-batch to continuous operation systems for wastewater treatment and protein recovery. This approach better aligns with conventional wastewater treatment modalities, improves scalability, and enhances resource recovery.

Analytical tools such as biomass characterization, digestibility testing, and feeding trials will be used to evaluate performance and optimize the process for maximum efficiency and nutritional value.

The methodology includes:

  • Design and optimization of anaerobic PNSB systems for continuous operation
  • Advanced biomass recovery and processing techniques
  • Comprehensive digestibility improvement studies
  • Rigorous aquaculture feeding trials with shrimp and sea bass
  • Techno-economic analysis and life cycle assessment

Research Methodology

Our systematic, multi-phase approach ensures comprehensive analysis and validation of the wastewater-to-aquafeed technology

01

System Design & Optimization

Development of continuous flow photobioreactor systems for purple non-sulfur bacteria cultivation, optimizing parameters for maximum biomass yield and protein content.

  • Photobioreactor configuration design
  • Parameter optimization (light, temperature, pH)
  • Biomass yield maximization
02

Process Characterization

Comprehensive analysis of biomass composition, nutrient profiles, and potential contaminants to ensure safety and nutritional adequacy for aquaculture applications.

  • Biomass composition analysis
  • Nutrient profiling
  • Contaminant screening
03

Digestibility Enhancement

Implementation of mechanical, enzymatic, and chemical treatments to improve biomass digestibility and nutrient bioavailability for target aquatic species.

  • Mechanical processing techniques
  • Enzymatic treatment optimization
  • Bioavailability assessment
04

Feed Formulation

Development of balanced feed formulations incorporating PNSB biomass at varying inclusion levels (30%, 50%, 70%) while maintaining nutritional completeness.

  • Formulation development (30%, 50%, 70% inclusion)
  • Nutritional balancing
  • Pellet quality testing
05

Aquaculture Trials

Rigorous feeding trials with shrimp (Penaeus vannamei) and sea bass (Dicentrarchus labrax) to evaluate growth performance, feed conversion ratios, and health parameters.

  • Shrimp (Penaeus vannamei) trials
  • Sea bass (Dicentrarchus labrax) trials
  • Growth performance analysis
06

Sustainability Assessment

Comprehensive life cycle assessment and techno-economic analysis to evaluate environmental impacts and economic viability of the proposed approach.

  • Life cycle assessment (LCA)
  • Techno-economic analysis
  • Environmental impact evaluation

Analytical Framework

Our methodology incorporates a comprehensive analytical framework to ensure rigorous evaluation of process efficiency, product quality, and environmental impact.

Advanced analytical techniques including HPLC, GC-MS, ICP-MS, and NIR spectroscopy will be employed to characterize biomass composition and monitor process parameters.

Key analytical components include:

  • Proximate analysis: Protein, lipid, carbohydrate, and ash content determination
  • Amino acid profiling: Complete amino acid composition using HPLC
  • Fatty acid analysis: Lipid profiling using GC-MS
  • Heavy metal screening: ICP-MS analysis for safety assessment
  • Digestibility studies: In vitro and in vivo assessment of nutrient availability
  • Water quality monitoring: Continuous assessment of wastewater treatment efficiency

Implementation Strategy

The project will be implemented through a phased approach, ensuring systematic progression from laboratory-scale optimization to pilot-scale validation.

Lab Scale Months 1-12
Pilot Scale Months 13-24
Trials Months 25-36
Analysis Months 37-42
01

Phase 1: Laboratory Scale

Months 1-12

Small-scale reactor optimization, parameter screening, and preliminary digestibility studies.

Key Activities:

  • Reactor design and optimization
  • Parameter screening (light, temperature, nutrients)
  • Preliminary digestibility studies
  • Biomass quality assessment

Expected Outputs:

Optimized reactor parameters Initial biomass characterization Digestibility baseline data
02

Phase 2: Pilot Scale

Months 13-24

Scale-up to pilot reactors, continuous process optimization, and feed formulation.

Key Activities:

  • Pilot reactor installation and operation
  • Continuous process optimization
  • Feed formulation development
  • Quality control protocols

Expected Outputs:

Pilot-scale production data Optimized feed formulations Quality control framework
03

Phase 3: Aquaculture Trials

Months 25-36

Controlled feeding trials, performance monitoring, and product quality assessment.

Key Activities:

  • Controlled feeding trials with target species
  • Growth performance monitoring
  • Health and nutritional assessment
  • Feed conversion ratio analysis

Expected Outputs:

Growth performance data Health assessment reports Feed efficiency metrics
04

Phase 4: Analysis & Reporting

Months 37-42

Data analysis, sustainability assessment, and comprehensive reporting.

Key Activities:

  • Comprehensive data analysis
  • Life cycle assessment
  • Techno-economic analysis
  • Final reporting and recommendations

Expected Outputs:

Final research report Sustainability assessment Implementation guidelines
